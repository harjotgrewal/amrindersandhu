My Story

Son of a farmer

I belong to a tiny village – Mishriwala, Punjab, India – where I was born and raised in a simple farming family. I learned to drive a tractor at the age of ten and helped my father in farming. Later, I moved between three different cities for my higher education.

Self-taught

In 2001, I designed my first website using Microsoft FrontPage. Before that, I used to paint on fabric and draw brand logos to perfection. My creativity and curiosity come from my mother. While getting started in design in 2007, I contacted a Yahoo designer who referred books and designers to learn from. I read more books in six months after college than during my whole school years.

Engineer turned designer

After completing my B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering, I started learning design on my own. Instead of joining Wipro technologies where I got placed during college, I opted for a freelance design career and never looked back.

And then a unicorn

As a freelancer, I had the choice to work on anything I wanted. Apart from designing apps, websites and blogs, my gigs included logos, brochures, business cards, pitch decks, posters, calendars, ads, stickers, and ebooks. Plus, I also developed a front-end architecture, style guides, and a design system.

Side project (Relpost)

While doing gigs during the early days of my career, I also started a side project in 2011. I designed, coded, and curated Relpost, which featured five best posts on a vital design topic.

Google, Intel, Groupon

Before joining Unmetric full-time in 2013, I got job offers from Google, Groupon, Intel, Zomato, MakeMyTrip, BookMyShow, etc. which I couldn’t accept because of relocation. And I have no regrets.

Helping a bedroom startup get acquired

After leading design at various startups and doing projects with Intel, Zomato, and Grasshopper, I joined Unmetric full-time. Before that, I was already working with Unmetric on a contract basis from day one, when our CEO was operating from his house in Chennai.

From 2010 till 2020, I lead the design team, did hands-on work to design three core products, website, blog, collateral, front-end architecture, and design system. Falcon.io acquired Unmetric in October 2019.

The story continues

I’m blessed and I have so much too be grateful for. My goal in life is to learn, share, care, and become a better person than I was yesterday.
