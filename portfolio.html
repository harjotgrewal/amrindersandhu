Front-end Development

Coding is a design tool to me. I have been hand-coding since the days of Internet Explorer 6, and when PSD to HTML conversions was a thing. Over the years, I have written HTML/CSS for everything from table-based newsletters, websites, and full-fledged web applications. During my ___ at Unmetric, I did front-end development for all the products, websites, blog and emails, introduced Gulp, Sass, Jade, BEM, built CSS architecture using concepts like OOCSS and SMACSS, and libraries like Compass and Bourbon. I also collaborated with Harry Roberts to improve the front-end codebase. Later on, along with mentoring two of our front-end developers, my responsibilities also included overseeing the front-end development and pitching the adoption of Angular to our engineering team. Just as architects should know the ins and outs of the building materials, similarly, designers working on the web should know the very languages that breathe life in the designs.

Team Alignment

Managing and working with remote teams is difficult. Being a remote designer from day 1, I have good amount of experience on things that can go sideways and ways to control and fix them. To improve communication at Unmetric, I started using Skype groups adding people working on the same project. Then came Slack and I was delighted. It took me more than __ to sell it internally and get people to quit Skype and switch to Slack. To understand business goals I sold OKRs to our CEO and created Google Sheets template so everyone can start using. Kickoffs, release checklists, steering slides, and monthly retrospectives were other major things that I introduced to help improve alignment and communication.

Content Strategy

Along with designing website and blog, I closely worked with our marketing team to do A/B testing. We tested different content strategy approaches like showcasing product benefits instead of features, replacing product pages with solutions, and adding Why Unmetric section that focuses on jobs that our customers perform and how Unmetric can help them do those jobs in a better way.

Product Strategy

Being a part of a product company, I contributed and influenced the product strategy on various occasions. We clearly defined our target audience (big brand and agencies), and also distinguished between choosers and users so we can cater to their needs better. I helped test a few ideas before we decided to commit more resources. By advocating and making customer research integral part of our product strategy, I helped our team discover needs of our customer and find a product-market fit.
